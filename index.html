Section 03

The Diagnostic Sequence

When an issue is flagged, the investigation follows this fixed sequence. No campaign setting is touched until all upstream layers are confirmed clean. This prevents the #1 agency mistake.

!

Issue Flagged

Alert triggered via dashboard anomaly, automated monitor, or manual observation

1

Platform Status

Is Google or Meta experiencing a known outage? Check status pages before doing anything else

2

Billing & Account Access

Payment method valid? Account active? Admin access intact?

3

Ad Approvals

Any disapprovals? Policy flags? Check all active ads for status changes

4

🎯 Tracking Verification — Critical Step

Open GTM Preview. Submit a test form. Does the conversion tag fire on submit — not page load? Is the event reaching Google/Meta? This single check resolves the majority of "campaign failures"

5

Landing Page

Is the page live? Loading under 3 seconds? Form functional? Offer still valid?

6

CRM Sync

Is SmileHub receiving leads? Are automations firing? Is the pipeline routing correctly?

7

Campaign Settings — Only Now

Bidding, targeting, audiences, creative. Only investigated after all upstream layers are confirmed clean

⚡

The Discipline That Separates Good from Average

A broken conversion tag is invisible from the outside. It looks exactly like a failing campaign. The instinct — touching campaign settings — makes it worse by feeding bad signals to an already-confused algorithm. Check tracking first, every time, without exception.

Root Cause Rule

Section 07

Creative Strategy for Dental & Ortho

Not all dental services convert the same way. Three distinct service segments require three distinct creative approaches. Generic "best dentist in [city]" messaging loses to practices that understand why patients convert.

High-Value / Considered

Invisalign · Implants · Veneers

  • Research cycle is long — awareness + retargeting layers required
  • Lead with outcomes: before/after journeys, patient video testimonials
  • Offer: free consultation, not "book treatment" — meet them where they are
  • Trust signals: doctor credentials, years experience, accreditations
  • Financing messaging ("from $X/month") removes cost anxiety
  • Meta: Lookalike from existing Invisalign patients. Google: branded + intent

Accessibility & Volume

Cleanings · Checkups · Whitening

  • Faster decision cycle — offer-led messaging works
  • Specificity beats aspiration: "Same-day appointments available" converts
  • Price anchoring where appropriate: "From $X, no insurance needed"
  • Urgency: limited slots, same-week availability
  • Target broad + geo-tightened within drive radius
  • Google Search > Meta for this tier — high intent queries

Teen Orthodontics

Braces · Clear Aligners · Retainers

  • Decision-maker is the parent — NOT the teenager
  • Target: Female, 32–48. Message to them, not to the teen
  • Back-to-school window (Aug–Sep) is peak — plan creative in July
  • Payment plan messaging: "Fits your family budget"
  • Board-certified orthodontist credentials prominent
  • Scheduling flexibility around school hours
Testing Structure

3 angles × 3 formats × 3 CTAs in rotation. Declare a winner only after statistical confidence — not gut feel or single-day results.

Frequency Rules

Meta frequency alert at 2.5. Hard restructure trigger at 3.0. Weekly check on all active ad sets. Documented learnings shared across team.

Quarterly Offer Audit

Every active offer reviewed: still valid? Still competitive? Still matched on the landing page? Discontinued offers killed immediately.

Dedicated Page Per Campaign

Homepage sends — no. Generic service pages — no. Every paid campaign needs a page that matches the ad offer word-for-word. Headline, sub, offer, CTA — exact match.

Mobile-First Build Standard

Over 70% of dental paid search traffic arrives on mobile. Design for the phone screen first. Form above fold on mobile is non-negotiable. Test on real devices, not Chrome DevTools.

Monthly Offer Alignment Check

Every active page checked monthly against the live offer. Any discrepancy flagged same day. Offer changes by the client trigger an immediate page update — this is a contractual requirement.

⚠ The Silent Failure Point

A practice discontinues an offer without telling the agency. The ad keeps running. The page still promises it. Conversion rate drops 30%+ over two weeks. No one knows why until the client calls. Prevention: offer change notification is contractual. Quarterly page audits happen regardless.

The 3-Day Rule

A metric must breach its threshold for three consecutive days before a campaign change is triggered. Single-day anomalies are monitored, not reacted to. One bad day is noise. Three consecutive days is a signal. This discipline prevents unnecessary churn in accounts that are actually performing fine.

Real-World Case Study

The Diagnosis That Defines the Approach

Auto King Australia — mobile mechanic network, Google Ads, QLD & NSW. I was brought in to turn around an account that had been steadily deteriorating. Here's exactly what I found — and why it matters for every dental account in this portfolio.

📋
The Situation

Account Deteriorating. Previous Fixes Not Working.

Auto King — mobile mechanic network operating across Queensland and New South Wales. Google Ads account, ~A$450/day. CPL had been inflating for months. Previous optimisation attempts — new bids, audience adjustments, creative swaps — had made no lasting difference. I was brought in to find out why.

🔍
The Instinct vs. The Discipline

Everyone Was Looking in the Wrong Place

The natural instinct — touching campaign settings. New match types. Different bidding. Swapped creative. That was the wrong move. Before touching a single campaign setting, I opened GTM Preview mode and submitted a test form.

Step four in the diagnostic sequence. Check tracking first. Every time.

⚠️
The Discovery

The Conversion Tag Did Not Fire.

The forms themselves were working. Leads were arriving. The client had no idea anything was wrong at the data layer. But from Google's perspective — this account had never recorded a single conversion.

A site rebuild had displaced the GTM container script. Common. Completely invisible unless you specifically check for it in GTM Preview mode.

⚡
The Pivot — Why This Changes Everything

The Algorithm Wasn't Failing. It Was Flying Blind.

For a significant period, Google's smart bidding algorithm had received zero conversion signals. So it did exactly what it's designed to do with no data — it optimised for what it could see: traffic volume, engagement, clicks. Not leads.

Impression share migrated toward lower-intent queries. Every optimisation the previous manager made — against corrupted data — had amplified the drift. They were retraining a confused algorithm with more noise.

✓
The Fix

Rebuilt From Clean Data Up

Rebuilt conversion tracking in GTM. Verified the full event chain end-to-end. Created a historical conversion data import to give the algorithm a corrected signal baseline. Then gradually transitioned to a lead-optimised bidding strategy from clean data.

Why This Is Every Dental Account's Risk

A CMS migration. A WordPress update. A GTM container republish. Any of these can silently break conversion tracking. The forms still work. Leads still come in. But the algorithm starts flying blind. CPL inflates. The client calls to say the ads stopped working. Everyone looks at campaigns, audiences, creative — when the answer is one GTM Preview check away.

The prevention is what you've seen across this entire playbook — systematic tracking verification in Pre-Launch QA, automated daily lead sync health checks, quarterly account audits. Not reactive troubleshooting. Proactive infrastructure.
